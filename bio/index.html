Vision

Research findings historically have taken years, even decades, to be fully reflected in practice. Even when research translates into change, it often takes the form of incremental improvements that leave larger, systemic issues unresolved.

My ultimate vision is for researchers to have clear relevance to today’s public policy decision-making, and for decision-makers to stay fully informed of cutting-edge research.

Mission

My professional passion is collaborating with scholars, governments, and communities to bridge knowledge and action.

I work with stakeholders on projects to generate new evidence that helps implement change in real-world policies, practices, and services. This involves coordinating across multiple points of view to identify priorities, design methodologies, and plan activities around sharing policy research.

I also lead the development of content and deliverables to meet knowledge-to-action needs. This involves crafting messages and plain language based on formal research findings, as well as creating visualizations to summarize and explain data.

Public policy serves a critical role in addressing issues like Indigenous cultural safety, institutional and structural racism, and climate justice. Research has a corresponding responsibility to promote and advance cultural relevance, holistic and wellness-oriented perspectives, and an upstream focus in our shared body of knowledge.
